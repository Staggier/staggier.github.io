The field of computer science is a vast one filled with many learning oppurtunities at every corner, throughout my years of studying at John Abbott College I have been able to learn many things inside and outside of my studies. One thing in particular that has stood out to me is my love for problem solving, there are very few things in life that bring me the satisfaction that finally getting over a tough problem would give me. With that in mind I have been able to really throw myself at any challenge that comes my way, regardless of its difficulties.

Something I have come to enjoy about Computer Sciences is the fact that its challenges come in many forms, and isn't limited in one area at all. Whether it's coming up with solutions for improving a website's quality of life, or fine tuning some aspect within a game, it's always something I'll find myself interested in and spending hours on for my own enjoyment. On top of that there's no better motivation than seeing my own progress as I learn and experience more challenges within the field.

Over the years I have tackled many kinds of problems on various problem solving websites such as

  • Project Euler
  • Kattis
  • HackerRank

and more, but there are a few problems that have stuck out to me and I've found really interesting to solve. My favorite problems have all come from Project Euler, which has a wide range of problems with varying difficulties. With such a vast amount of problems with overlapping areas, it's always fun to continue learning and then profiting from that learning when tackling other problems.
